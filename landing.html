The Game

HOW IT WORKS

Studio Exec Fantasy is a season-long competition where each Exec manages a slate of films. Execs compete at the box office and at the awards circuit to prove they have the best slate in the league.

Draft Your Slate

Each exec starts with a budget and bids on films releasing in the Oscar-to-Oscar window. Any film releasing in that window is fair game. Tent poles, prestige, wild cards.

AUCTION DRAFT

Earn at the Box Office

Every dollar your films earn at the domestic box office goes directly onto your scoreboard. A $10M bid on a $200M hit? That's some ROI.

LIVE TRACKING

Stack Awards

Oscar nominations and wins convert to bonus earnings at the end of the season. Best Picture is worth $50M. Every other win or nomination adds $25M each.

The Fine Print

THE RULES

The Draft Window

Any film releasing between Oscar Sunday and Oscar Sunday the following year is eligible. The window is open. Draft anything.

Festival Futures

Execs can bid on the winner of Cannes, TIFF's People's Choice, and other major festivals before the winner is announced. High risk, high reward.

Unspent Budget Pays Out

Every dollar you don't spend in the draft rolls into your final total. Savvy budgeting is a real competitive edge. Don't overbid.

Winner Takes the Pot

Highest total earnings at the end of the Oscar season wins everything. Box office plus Oscar bonuses plus unspent budget. The exec with the best eye wins it all.

Voids

Some picks get voided and the money does not come back. A film moves outside the Oscar-to-Oscar window: that exec loses the film and the spend. A festival future winner was already drafted by another exec: that exec loses the money and gets no film. Voids are part of the game. This industry is risky, this game is no different.
